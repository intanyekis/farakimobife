    Quentin Tarantino made his directorial debut back in 1992 with the film Reservoir Dogs. Since then, the esteemed film director has been the mastermind behind some of the industry's most iconic films from Kill Bill, Pulp Fiction, and Once Upon A Time In Hollywood, which marks one of Tarantino's final films.

    The Hollywood legend has had his fair share of ups and downs throughout his career, however, he's always been praised by critics and of course the Academy when it comes to his many accolades.

    While the film director has revealed what one of his favorite films of his is, Quentin has also shared which of his is his worst. Although it might be hard to think of a bad Tarantino film, the two-time Oscar-winning director managed to come up with one!

    Quentin Tarantino's Worst Film

    Quentin Tarantino went from working in a video store back in California to becoming one of the most commercially successful film directors in history, making it clear he's quite the legend! The star has left quite the mark when it comes to independent films, cementing his status in Hollywood for like...ever!

    Quentin Tarantino made his directorial debut back in 1992, however, it didn't take too much time before he was working with major names including Uma Thurman, Brad Pitt, Samuel L. Jackson, and John Travolta, to name a few. Throughout his career, Tarantino has stood as the mastermind behind some of his most incredible pieces such as Pulp Fiction, Kill Bill, Django Unchained, and Once Upon A Time In Hollywood, to name a few, scoring himself 8 Oscar nominations and 2 wins!

    Despite having a plethora of masterpieces under his belt, Quentin Tarantino isn't a fan of all of his own movies! According to the director himself, one of his least favorite films is Death Proof, which was a part of the double-feature film, Grindhouse, a movie he shares with Robert Rodriguez.

    Tarantino shared his opinions regarding Death Proof during his interview with The Hollywood Reporter, claiming that "Death Proof has got to be the worst movie I ever made! And for a left-handed movie, that wasn't so bad, all right? So, if that's the worst I ever get, I'm good," Quentin said.

    The director made it clear that for him, it's "all about the filmography", and while he's created wonders in the past, Death Proof just isn't one of them, that is in Mr. Tarantino's opinion, as many fans disagree. Although Quentin isn't a fan, the film has a score of nearly 70% on Rotten Tomatoes, making it evident that it's still very much a fan favorite, despite being Quentin Tarantino's least!

    Quentin Tarantino's Retirement

    When it was announced that Quentin Tarantino would be directing Once Upon A Time In Hollywood, which starred major names such as Brad Pitt, Leonardo DiCaprio, Margot Robbie, and Al Pacino, it was also revealed that this would be one of Quentin's last films!

    Although he isn't stepping down just yet, Quentin Tarantino made it clear that after nearly 4 decades in the business, he would officially be retiring. Fans began to speculate what his final film would entail, and just when he'd go on to release it, however, Tarantino is keeping fans on their toes, and rightfully so, considering Once Upon A Time is still a hot film.

    When it comes to his work in the spotlight, the actor has directed countless hit movies, however, Tarantino shared this year that he will officially be done after his tenth movie! Although he hasn't given a true reason why he'll be leaving Hollywood, fans are certain it's to finally put his mind to rest, and we're certain he needs it.
